Project Description

Living Rights Legacy presents a map timeline of select exhibits from the Canadian Museum of Human Rights. Our motivations are to bring awareness and contribute to the conversation of human rights violations across the world and create an entry point for the museumâ€™s contents on the subject. The intellectual significance is to highlight where human rights started prior to the Declaration from the United Nations in 1948 and where we are now, by supplying a visual and auditory display of some key events that have happened in human rights history and exhibits featured in the Canadian Museum of Human Rights. The importance of this project is that the exhibits are from the perspectives of survivors, favoring and centering their voices and sharing how exploitative and dehumanizing these actions are and have been.
