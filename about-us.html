Rylee LaLonde

Rylee LaLonde is a first year doctoral student studying Anthropology. Her research interests include Native American and Indigenous Studies, Community-Based Research, Human Rights Investigations, and Rights and Reclamation.

Jada Gannaway

Jada Gannaway is a fifth-year doctoral candidate studying Caribbean History. Gannaway’s research interests include Black Left politics, Black women’s history, and digital pedagogy.

Morgan Hill

Morgan Hill is a third-year MFA candidate focused in Studio Arts. Hill’s research documents physical rituals that form communal bonds in black culture and other marginalized communities. The output develops into abstract art that is informed by black sensibility, illegibility, and the right to opacity.

Madiha Ghous

Madiha Ghous is Ruth & Yunck Endowed Scholar and Doctoral Candidate in the Department of English and Film Studies at the Michigan State University. Her teaching and research interests revolve around the areas of Post & Decolonial studies, Global Anglophone Literatures, Cultural Cold War Studies, Critical Muslim Studies and South-Asia Studies.
